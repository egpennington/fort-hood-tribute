General Leonard Wood

The man who shaped the United States's military


Here is a timeline of Col Leonard Wood's life:

  • Born - October 9, 1860
  • 1884 - Graduated Harvard Medical School
  • 1885 - Joined Army as assistant surgeon Dept. of Arizona
  • 1898 - Recieved Medal Of Honor
  • 1903 - Promoted to Major General
  • 1903 - Spanish-American War

    • Wood and Roosevelt organized the 1st Volunteer Cavalry Regiment, popularly known as the Rough Riders

  • 1906 - Commander of Philippines Division
  • 1910 - Named Army Chief of Staff

    • Wood implemented several programs, among which were the forerunner of the Reserve Officers' Training Corps (ROTC) program, and the Preparedness Movement, a campaign for universal military training and wartime conscription. The Preparedness Movement led to implementation of the Selective Service System shortly before World War I.


    "The strength of right must be organized against the day when it may be necessary to meet the forces of wrong." - Col Leonard Wood

  • 1913 - Commander of the Army's Eastern Department
  • 1915 - published as a pro-preparedness book, The Military Obligation of Citizenship
  • 1919 - WW1 Distinguished Service Medal and the Legion of Honor

    • After the war, Wood was appointed to command the Sixth Corps Area, which he led from 1919 to 1921.

  • 1921 - Retired from the U.S. Army

    • Then appointment as Governor General of the Philippines

  • 1927 - Dies undergoing surguery for a brain tumor

    • The successful removal of Wood's first brain tumor in 1910 represented an important milestone, indicating to the public the advances that had been made in the nascent field of neurosurgery, and extending Wood's life by almost two decades. His brain is held at the Yale University School of Medicine as part of an historic collection of Harvey Cushing's patients' preserved brains.

  • 1940 - Fort Leonard Wood named in his honor

    • Also one of the U.S. Navy's World War II-era Harris-class attack transports, USS Leonard Wood (APA-12), was named for Wood.
